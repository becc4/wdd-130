Overview

Purpose

I want a way to showcase my work for potential employers as well as record my progress in my studies. To do this, I will create a resume website that contains personal projects and major school assignments related to computer science. It will be itself an example of my skill sets. As I get better and work on more projects, I will be able to add to it. This was a method recommended to me by professionals in computer science as a resume but also to encourage personal projects.

Audience

This website is mostly for me. It is a place I can keep my work and accomplishments, something to work to contribute to. Others who may enjoy this are friends and family who are curious about what I am working on and learning. The audience I will be catering to, however, are potential employers. This website portfolio will be a way for them to see what I am capable of to detailed, greater length than a resume.

Content

Home page

Career Objectives I have goals to succeed in life, and thus in a career. Programming has provided that as a study that fills in all the gaps. I have always been logic-minded. Learning to program taught me how to continue practicing the logic-based skills required in high school, allowing me to enjoy them in my college studies and in my adult career. I find joy in solving problems and I have found a career path that involves just that. I still do not have firm plans on what I will do in my adult career, and upon the advice of professors, this study is very fluid and has intersections between its many paths. I am currently set on becoming a software developer, with most of the professional people I’ve spoken to working as one. Mission Statement My career objectives, alongside doing programming in a work-setting, is to be an influence. I grew up in a district that prided themselves on teaching new fields like programming. I had attended many classes from middle school up about basic programming, classes designed more to get students interested than to teach foundational skills. However, it was only when I had a female instructor in a college course teaching programming did I finally realize that it was something I had wanted to do. I knew I could be good at it if I tried, but there was some subconscious block that told me women weren’t allowed to be programmers, somehow made worse by field trips to museums to learn about women in STEM. They made it seem unordinary and unnatural which could only be reversed by seeing actual women in STEM, existing and enjoying their chosen study. I want to be a visible example to girls that it is not unusual to be a female programmer. It is a field to be enjoyed by women if they can find joy in it. About Me Societies I am a current member in the Society of Women Engineers (SWE) at Brigham Young University - Idaho (BYU-I). There, I have participated in school-hosted and society-hosted Hack-A-Thons where I collaborated with team members and exponded my learning, particularly in Computer Systems which helped me for my class in it this semester. Hobbies I enjoy writing and have for seven years. This has led me to minor in English, and has exposed me to a variety of ideas that have challenged and strengthened my own. I practice argumentative writing, using different critical theories to analyze literature both for class and during my freetime. I also like creative writing, practicing it for seven years. I was a part of my high school’s Poetry Club and enjoy writing poetry, but my biggest strength is prose work. My logic-based mind has helped my writing become more structured which has made me a better planned story-teller.
